McCall Fire Causes Loss Of $40,000

Idaho Statesman March 31, 1942

Early Morning Blaze Destroys Yacht Club And Four Businesses

Volunteer fire fighters early Monday morning battled a blaze that destroyed a building housing the Yacht club and four other McCall business locations with loss estimated at $40,000.

Besides the club, with a boathouse containing 20 motorboats, a bowling alley, beauty shop, sporting goods store and a cabinet maker's shop were destroyed.

Started in Basement
Mrs. E. J. Peabody, owner of the building, reported the fire at 5:30 a.m. The blaze, apparently originating in the basement, was then out of control and by the time fire fighting equipment could be brought to the scene, flames prevented removal of anything from the building

The estimated loss included the Yacht club facilities operated by Riley Joy and Roy Smythe of Boise; beauty shop operated by Margaret Gribble; Sportland recreation arena, a bowling alley, soda fountain and the H. G. Low cabinet shop. Mrs. Peabody placed the value of the building, a stucco-covered structure built of ties, at $8,000. It was not insured.

Fourth Fire
It was the fourth disastrous fire In the McCall business district in nearly four years since the McCall hotel, one of the Payette Lakes resort town's largest hostelries, was destroyed by flames.

Another fire destroyed the Brown Tie and Lumber mill causing $100,000 loss, and still another made ashes of an entire business block. The hotel and mill have been restored, and several of the Main street structures have been replaced.

Motorboats known to have been in the Yacht club building when the fire struck included those belonging to Art Jones, Cecil Jordan, Lester F. Albert, Bessle Falt, Clem W. Gamble, Al A. Fraser, J. L. Driscoll, Roy Smythe ana Riley Joy, all of Boise, and Van Patten of Ontario.
